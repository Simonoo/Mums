A fine line between abstraction and figuration underpins my work, the concept of time and place, movement and light, glimpse and memory inspires my paintings which I work on in my studio or to finish work on paintings I have started ‘plein air’.

I use the coast line of North Wales as inspiration for my work in oils, watercolour and mixed media. For heavier mixed media work I use sand, seaweed and perhaps driftwood giving texture and interesting mark making to the paint as it runs and blurs giving a feeling of working with nature.

Each day I draw, take photographs, collect interesting beach materials which I can draw spiration from I like to capture light, atmosphere, illusion, infinity and reflection.

My latest work is with encaustic medium, the word encaustic from the Greek word ‘enkaustkos’ meaning burnt in. The oldest well known encaustic paintings are the Fayum portraits of Roman Egypt they are among the best preserved paintings from ancient times, Some of these paintings can be seen in St Catherine’s Church Mount Sinai, The British Museum and a few other museums in the world, still with vibrant colours surviving thousands of years.
