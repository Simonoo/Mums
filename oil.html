My work revolves around the coastline where I live and walk every day. A fine line between abstraction and figuration underpins my work, the concepts of time and place, movement and light, glimpse and memory inspires my paintings.

I use oil paint on canvas and paper sometimes mixed media for heavier mark making and texture. I like to use sand if it finds it's way onto the canvas on a breezy day, seaweed, tiny pieces of driftwood and shell giving interesting marks in the paint as it runs and blurs, a feeling of interacting with nature.

'Plein Air'work is finished in my studio from photographs I have taken to capture the light or atmosphere, sometimes just from memory. I paint images of illusion, infinity and reflection.

My latest works are with 'encaustic' medium, wax, resin and pigments on board. Ecaustic from the greek word 'enkaustkos' meaning burnt in, the oldest well known paintings are the Fayum portraits of Roman Egypt and are among the best preserved paintings from ancient times.
