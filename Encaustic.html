Researching the cold wax medium I became interested in encaustic, hot beeswax and resin; it is perfect for using with oils and dry pigments. Researching the history of encaustic I started to copy the old panels from St Catherine’s, Mount Sinai, ’ Icons of the first recorded paintings of Christ’ and also the Fayum portraits some of which are still found in The British Museum and other museums in the world - still in vibrant colour which have survived thousands of years dating back almost 3000 yrs.

The Fayum portraits were mainly painted to hang on walls and then after death the portraits were bound into the mummy wrappings showing the person in life.

Although an abstract artist I found it interesting to copy the portraits and followed on with Pre Raphaelite and later works, alongside my own work of abstract panels.
